Roof Repair or Roof Replacement?

Most roofs we look at fall clearly into one bucket. Here's how to tell which one yours is.

Path A

When to Repair

  • Damage is in one defined area — a missing patch, one leak, one popped flashing.
  • The rest of the roof is under 15 years old and shows no granule loss.
  • Underlying decking is dry and solid when we open the area up.
  • Attic is dry; no water staining showing up across multiple ceilings.
  • You're not selling, refinancing, or filing a comprehensive insurance claim soon.
Path B

When to Replace

  • Damage shows up in multiple areas across the field of the roof.
  • Granules are accumulating in your gutters; shingles look bald in spots.
  • You've got two or more interior ceiling stains in different rooms.
  • The roof is 20+ years old (asphalt) or showing systemic rust/separation (metal).
  • A storm hit hard enough that an insurance claim is in motion.
  • Decking is soft, sagging, or has visible rot when we open it up.

Not sure? Free roof inspection — we'll tell you straight which one your roof needs.
