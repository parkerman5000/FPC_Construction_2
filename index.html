Renovate or Rebuild from the Ground Up?

Two roads. We'll walk yours and tell you which one your project actually needs — straight, no upsell.

Path A

When to Renovate

  • The structure (foundation, framing, roof deck) is sound — only finishes or one system have failed.
  • You're updating one or two rooms and want to keep your existing footprint.
  • You can stay in the home or building during the work.
  • Permits and zoning are on your side for the change you have in mind.
  • Budget is best spent on visible upgrades — kitchen, bath, fencing, driveway — not hidden ones.
Path B

When to Rebuild from the Ground Up

  • The foundation has settled, cracked, or is failing in more than one spot.
  • The roof, plumbing, and electrical are all near end-of-life — patching one only exposes the next.
  • You need a layout the existing footprint can't give you (square footage, ceiling height, zoning use).
  • Storm, fire, or moisture damage runs across multiple systems.
  • The land needs site prep — clearing, grading, septic — that's easier with a clean slate.

Not sure? Talk to Fil. We do free walk-throughs and we'll tell you straight which one your situation calls for.
